Chicken Yakisoba

Description:

My favorite dish! Yakisoba is a japanese dish made around 1950, after World War 2. It is mainly made of noodles, vegetables and Worcestershire sauce. This variation involves chicken, a protein source that makes Yakisoba even more delicious!

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon canola oil
  • 2 tablespoons chile paste
  • 2 cloves garlic, chopped
  • 4 skinless, boneless chicken breasts halves - cut into 1 inch cubes
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce (worcestershire sauce if not available)
  • 1 onion, sliced lengthwise into eighths
  • 1/2 medium head cabbage,coarsely chopped
  • 2 carrots, coarsely chopped
  • 8 ounces soba noodles, cooked and drained

Steps:

  1. In a large skillet, combine sesame oil, canola oil chili paste and stir-fry for 30 seconds
  2. Add garlic and stir fry an additional 30 seconds
  3. Add chicken and 1/4 cup of soy sauce and stir fry until chicken is no longer pink (about 5 minutes)
  4. Remove mixture from pan, set aside and keep warm
  5. In the emptied pan combine the onion, cabbage and carrots
  6. Stir-fry until cabbage begins to wilt (2 to 3 minutes)
  7. In the emptied pan combine the onion, cabbage and carrots
  8. Stir-fry until cabbage begins to wilt, 2 to 3 minutes
  9. Stir in the remaining soy sauce, cooked noodles and the chicken mixture to pan and mix to blend
  10. Enjoy!
